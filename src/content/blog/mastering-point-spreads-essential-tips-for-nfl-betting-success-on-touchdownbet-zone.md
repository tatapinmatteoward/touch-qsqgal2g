## What Exactly is a Point Spread?

In simple terms, a point spread is a handicap applied to the favorite team in a matchup to level the playing field for betting purposes. Instead of just picking a winner, you're betting on the margin of victory. For example:

- If the Kansas City Chiefs are favored over the Las Vegas Raiders by 7 points (Chiefs -7), they need to win by more than 7 points for your bet to win.
- If the Raiders lose by 6 points or fewer (or win outright), the underdog covers the spread.
